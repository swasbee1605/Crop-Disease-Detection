About the Disease

Tomato early blight is a serious disease caused by fungus Alternaria solani.

Recommended Actions

  • Use resistant tomato varieties.
  • Apply fungicides like chlorothalonil or copper-based products early in the season.
  • Rotate crops and avoid planting tomatoes in the same area consecutively.
  • Ensure proper spacing to improve air circulation and reduce humidity.
{% elif dis.prediction == 'Tomato leaf bacterial spot' %}

About the Disease

Tomato leaf bacterial spot is caused by Botrytis cinerea.

Recommended Actions

  • Remove affected leaves and maintain good airflow around plants.
  • Apply fungicides such as copper-based or sulfur-based sprays.
  • Ensure proper spacing and avoid wetting the leaves during watering.
{% elif dis.prediction == 'Tomato leaf late blight' %}

About the Disease

Tomato leaf late blight is caused by Fungus Phytophthora infestans.

Recommended Actions

  • Use fungicides like mancozeb or chlorothalonil.
  • Remove and destroy infected plants to prevent the spread.
  • Avoid overhead irrigation and ensure proper spacing for air circulation.
{% elif dis.prediction == 'Tomato leaf mosaic virus' %}

About the Disease

Tomato leaf mosaic virus is caused by insects like aphids.

Recommended Actions

  • Remove and destroy infected plants as there is no cure.
  • Control insect vectors (like aphids) using insecticidal soaps or neem oil.
  • Use resistant varieties and sanitize tools to prevent virus spread.
{% elif dis.prediction == 'Tomato leaf yellow virus' %}

About the Disease

Tomato leaf yellow virus is caused by whiteflies.

Recommended Actions

  • Control whitefly populations using insecticidal soaps or reflective mulches.
  • Use resistant varieties to prevent infection.
