Tomato Early Blight

Symptoms: Dark, concentric rings on older leaves leading to yellowing and leaf drop.

Treatment: Apply fungicides like chlorothalonil, remove and destroy infected plant debris.

Tomato Leaf Late Blight

Symptoms: Dark, water-soaked patches on leaves and stems, often spreading rapidly in wet conditions.

Treatment: Fungicides like mancozeb or chlorothalonil, remove and destroy infected plants.

Tomato Leaf Mosaic Virus

Symptoms: Mottled, yellow or green mosaic patterns on leaves, leaves may become deformed.

Treatment: Remove infected plants, disinfect tools, control aphid populations.

Tomato Leaf Yellow Virus

Symptoms: Yellowing of leaves, stunted growth, and reduced fruit size.

Treatment: Control whitefly vectors with insecticides, remove infected plants.

Tomato Mold Leaf

Symptoms: White to gray moldy patches, often on the undersides of leaves, leading to wilting.

Treatment: Improve air circulation, apply sulfur-based fungicides, remove infected plant parts.

Tomato Septoria Leaf Spot

Symptoms: Small, circular spots with dark brown margins and gray centers, leading to leaf drop.

Treatment: Apply copper fungicides, avoid overhead watering, remove infected leaves.

Tomato Leaf Bacterial Spot

Symptoms: Small water-soaked spots that turn brown, with yellow halos around the lesions, leaf yellowing.

Treatment: Copper-based fungicides, remove and destroy affected leaves or plants.
